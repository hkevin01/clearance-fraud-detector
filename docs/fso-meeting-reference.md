# FSO Meeting Reference Card — Exact Regulatory Paragraphs

> **Use this during your meeting. Every quote below is the verbatim regulatory text with a direct link to the official source.**  
> Last verified: April 11, 2026 against ecfr.gov (32 CFR Part 117 last amended 3/09/2026).

---

## 1. When can an FSO collect your SSN / initiate the investigation?

**ANSWER: Only after a written job offer AND written acceptance — no exceptions.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(f)(1):**  
> *"If a potential employee requires access to classified information immediately upon commencement of employment, the contractor **may submit a request for investigation prior to the date of employment**, provided:*  
> *(i) A written commitment for employment has been made by the contractor.*  
> *(ii) The candidate has accepted the offer in writing."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(f)

**What this means for you:** Even in the earliest permissible scenario (pre-employment investigation), BOTH a written offer AND written acceptance must already exist. A cold-call FSO asking for SSN before any offer has been made is outside this authority entirely.

---

## 2. What is the FSO allowed to do with your SF-86 data?

**ANSWER: Review it for completeness only — cannot share it or use it for any other purpose.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(d)(1):**  
> *"Provide the employee with written notification that review of the SF 86 by the FSO or other contractor employee is **for adequacy and completeness** and information will be used for **no other purpose** within the entity."*

> **32 CFR § 117.10(d)(2):**  
> *"Not share information from the employee's SF 86 within the entity and will **not use the information for any purpose other than** determining the adequacy and completeness of the SF 86."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(d)

**What this means for you:** An FSO who collects your SSN and then uses it for anything other than submitting the SF-86 to the CSA is violating this paragraph.

---

## 3. What is the authorized system for SF-86 submission?

**ANSWER: e-QIP or its successor system (NBIS eApp at eapp.nbis.mil). Not email. Not phone.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(d):**  
> *"The electronic version of the SF 86...must be completed in **e-QIP or its successor system** by the contractor employee and reviewed by the FSO."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(d)

**NBIS eApp (successor to e-QIP):** https://eapp.nbis.mil  
**DCSA confirmation:** https://www.dcsa.mil/Systems-Applications/National-Background-Investigation-Services-NBIS/NBIS-eApp-NBIS-Agency/

**What this means for you:** Your SSN goes into eApp — only. Any FSO asking you to email, text, or verbally provide SSN instead of directing you to eApp is not following § 117.10(d).

---

## 4. How does clearance transfer/reciprocity work? Who provides the SSN?

**ANSWER: The CONTRACTOR provides the SSN to the new CSA for verification — not the candidate verbally to the FSO.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(6):**  
> *"The contractor will provide the new CSA with the full name, date, and place of birth, social security number, clearing agency, and type of investigation for **verification**."*

> **32 CFR § 117.10(h):**  
> *"The applicable CSA is responsible for determining whether contractor employees have been previously determined eligible for access to classified information or investigated by an authorized investigative activity in accordance with SEAD 7."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(6)  
**SEAD 7 (Reciprocity):** https://www.dni.gov/files/NCSC/documents/Regulations/SEAD-7_BI_ReciprocityU.pdf

**What this means for you:** In a clearance transfer, the FSO at the gaining contractor submits your information (from the formal application you already submitted) to DCSA — no one calls you and asks for your SSN to "run a reciprocity check."

---

## 5. How does DISS fit in? Can an FSO look you up before an offer?

**ANSWER: FSOs use DISS via their own credentialed login. Any SSN in DISS is already on file from a prior investigation. There is no legitimate process where an FSO cold-calls a candidate to get SSN "to search DISS."**

> **DCSA DISS page (dcsa.mil):**  
> *"Joint Verification System (JVS) enables DoD professionals to document security clearance access and **verify eligibility determinations**."*

**DCSA source:** https://www.dcsa.mil/Systems-Applications/Defense-Information-System-for-Security-DISS/

**Training authority:** CDSE (Center for Development of Security Excellence) — https://www.cdse.edu/Training/eLearning/  
FSO DISS credentialing is through STEPP: https://cdse.ualearning.gov

**What this means for you:** A DISS lookup is something the FSO does in their own system using information already on record — not something they need you to supply over the phone.

---

## 6. What governs how eligibility is adjudicated?

**ANSWER: SEAD 4 — the 13 National Security Adjudicative Guidelines, applied by DCSA.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(2):**  
> *"The CSA will determine eligibility for access to classified information **in accordance with SEAD 4**."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(2)  
**SEAD 4 full text:** https://www.dni.gov/files/NCSC/documents/Regulations/SEAD-4-Adjudicative-Guidelines-U.pdf  
**DCSA Adjudications page:** https://www.dcsa.mil/mc/pv/adjudications/adjudicative-guidelines/

**What this means for you:** Only DCSA applies these guidelines to make a clearance determination. No employer, recruiter, or FSO can pre-determine, guarantee, or expedite that decision.

---

## 7. What is FIPS 201-3 and does it apply to your clearance investigation?

**ANSWER: FIPS 201-3 governs PIV/CAC card issuance — it is a separate process from security clearance investigation.**

> **FIPS 201-3, Section 1 (Purpose):**  
> *"This document establishes a standard for a Personal Identity Verification (PIV) system that meets the control and security objectives of Homeland Security Presidential Directive-12 (HSPD-12). It is based on secure and reliable forms of identity credentials issued by the Federal Government to its employees and contractors."*

**FIPS 201-3:** https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/fips/201-3/final  
**PDF:** https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/FIPS/NIST.FIPS.201-3.pdf

**What this means for you:** FIPS 201-3 is about your CAC/PIV smart card. The identity proofing it describes (in-person, at a credentialing office) is for issuing the physical card — not for collecting SSN over the phone to "verify" a clearance.

---

## 8. Is CBSV (SSA's Consent Based SSN Verification Service) used by FSOs?

**ANSWER: No. SSA explicitly states CBSV is for commercial banking/mortgage companies and does NOT verify identity for DoD purposes.**

> **SSA CBSV page (ssa.gov/cbsv):**  
> *"CBSV does not verify identity, citizenship, employment eligibility, nor does it interface with the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) verification system... CBSV is typically used by companies that provide **banking and mortgage services**, process credit checks, provide background checks, satisfy licensing requirements."*

**SSA source:** https://www.ssa.gov/cbsv/

**What this means for you:** Any claim that an FSO is using "SSN verification" via CBSV, or any SSA service, to verify your clearance is false. DoD clearance verification runs through DISS/JVS — an FSO-credentialed DoD system, not an SSA commercial API.

---

## 9. How does an FSO verify a candidate's clearance level? What system? What information?

**ANSWER: Only the FSO (not HR, not a recruiter) can look up clearance status — and only via DISS JVS, using a credentialed DoD CAC login, after a written offer exists.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(6):**  
> *"The contractor will not submit a request for an eligibility determination to one CSA if the employee applicant is known to be cleared or in process for eligibility for access to classified information by another CSA. In such cases, **reciprocity of eligibility determination in accordance with SEAD 7** shall be used. The contractor will provide the new CSA with the full name, date, and place of birth, **social security number**, clearing agency, and type of investigation for **verification**."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(6)

**What the FSO actually does:** Logs into DISS Joint Verification System (JVS) at `dissportal.nbis.mil` using their CAC-credentialed government account → searches by name + SSN + DOB → system returns clearance level, investigation tier, adjudication date, and access status from the federal record already on file from a prior investigation.

**DISS JVS login:** https://dissportal.nbis.mil (DoD CAC required — FSO only)  
**DCSA DISS page:** https://www.dcsa.mil/Systems-Applications/Defense-Information-System-for-Security-DISS/

> **DCSA DISS page (verbatim):**  
> *"Joint Verification System (JVS) enables DoD professionals to **document security clearance access and verify eligibility determinations**."*

---

## 10. Can a recruiter or HR person verify your clearance before an offer?

**ANSWER: No. Recruiters and HR have zero access to any federal clearance database. The only verification happens through the FSO, only after a written offer, only in DISS.**

> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(5):**  
> *"The contractor will limit requests for determinations of eligibility for access to classified information to the **minimum number of employees and consultants necessary** for operational efficiency in accordance with contractual obligations... Requests for determinations of eligibility for access to classified information **will not be used to establish a cache of cleared employees**."*

> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(7):**  
> *"Contractors **will not submit requests** for determination of eligibility for access to classified information for individuals who are **not their employees or consultants**; nor will they submit requests for employees of subcontractors."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(5)

**What this means for you:** It is a regulatory violation to run a clearance verification (or initiate an investigation) on anyone who is not already a contractor employee or consultant. A staffing firm or recruiter with no active contract relationship cannot legally initiate or run your clearance check. Any person claiming to "verify your clearance" before an offer letter exists is either lying about what they're doing or violating §117.10(a)(7).

---

## 11. Before requesting a new investigation, what must the FSO check first?

**ANSWER: CVS (Central Verification System) — to see if an existing investigation already satisfies the requirement (reciprocity). This is the mandatory first step.**

> **DCSA CVS page (verbatim):**  
> *"Before requesting an investigation, agencies should check the **Central Verification System (CVS)** to determine if there is an existing adjudication or investigation that meets the current need. CVS is designated as the **primary tool for facilitating reciprocal decisions**, as required by Executive Orders, regulations, and policies."*

**DCSA CVS source:** https://www.dcsa.mil/Systems-Applications/Central-Verification-System-CVS/

> **32 CFR § 117.10(h)(1):**  
> *"Any current eligibility determination for access to classified information that is based on an investigation of a scope that meets or exceeds that necessary for the required level of access will **provide the basis for a new eligibility determination**."*

> **32 CFR § 117.10(h)(2):**  
> *"The prior investigation will be used **without further investigation or adjudication** unless the CSA becomes aware of significant derogatory information that was not previously adjudicated."*

**Direct link:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(h)  
**SEAD 7 (Reciprocity policy):** https://www.dni.gov/files/NCSC/documents/Regulations/SEAD-7_BI_ReciprocityU.pdf

**What this means for you:** If you already hold an active clearance at the required level, the FSO may not need to submit a new investigation at all. They look in CVS first. If reciprocity applies, you do not fill out eApp again — your existing record transfers. This is why your SSN is not "collected" for verification — it's already in the federal system from your prior investigation.

---

## 12. What are the three phases of clearance vetting and who does what?

| Phase | Who | System | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Initial screening / interview | HR / Recruiter | **None** — no database access | Candidate self-reports; recruiter has zero authority or system access to verify |
| Post-offer clearance verification | **FSO only** (credentialed) | DISS JVS at `dissportal.nbis.mil` | 32 CFR §117.10(a)(6), (h); SEAD 7 |
| Reciprocity check before new investigation | FSO + DCSA | CVS (primary) + DISS | 32 CFR §117.10(h)(1)-(2); DCSA CVS page |
| New investigation needed | FSO initiates → you complete | NBIS eApp at `eapp.nbis.mil` | 32 CFR §117.10(d), (f) |

**Key point to make to FSO:** SSN is never legitimately "asked for" in Phase 1. It enters the federal system once — when you complete your first SF-86 via eApp. Every subsequent verification by every subsequent FSO uses that existing federal record. Cold-soliciting SSN outside of the eApp process has no regulatory basis.

## VIOLATIONS — Exact Rules Broken When SSN Is Solicited Pre-Offer

> **Use this section to state which specific rule is being violated when someone asks for your SSN outside the authorized process.**

---

### VIOLATION 1 — Initiating investigation before written offer and written acceptance

**Rule broken:**
> **32 CFR § 117.10(f)(1) — Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 117, Section 10, Paragraph (f), Subparagraph (1)**
>
> *"If a potential employee requires access to classified information immediately upon commencement of employment, the contractor may submit a request for investigation prior to the date of employment, **provided:***
>
> *(i) A written commitment for employment has been made by the contractor.*
>
> *(ii) The candidate has accepted the offer in writing."*

**Document:** Code of Federal Regulations — Title 32 (National Defense), Part 117 (NISPOM)  
**Section:** § 117.10 — "Determination of eligibility for access to classified information for contractor employees"  
**Paragraph:** (f) — "Pre-employment eligibility determination action"  
**Subsection:** (f)(1)(i) and (f)(1)(ii)  
**Direct URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(f)

**What is violated:** Collecting SSN or submitting any investigation request before BOTH conditions (i) and (ii) are satisfied is outside the authority granted by this paragraph. There is no earlier permissible trigger — not "preliminary interest," not "pipeline vetting," not "clearance confirmation." The written offer and written acceptance are the **only** gate that opens the process.

---

### VIOLATION 2 — Submitting a clearance request for a non-employee

**Rule broken:**
> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(7) — Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 117, Section 10, Paragraph (a), Subparagraph (7)**
>
> *"Contractors **will not submit requests** for determination of eligibility for access to classified information for individuals who are **not their employees or consultants**; nor will they submit requests for employees of subcontractors."*

**Document:** 32 CFR Part 117 (NISPOM)  
**Section:** § 117.10 — "Determination of eligibility for access to classified information for contractor employees"  
**Paragraph:** (a) — "General"  
**Subsection:** (a)(7)  
**Direct URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(7)

**What is violated:** A person who has not yet been hired is not an employee. Any clearance-related action (investigation request, DISS lookup initiated under false pretense, SSN collection framed as clearance verification) taken against a job candidate before the employment relationship is formalized violates this paragraph.

---

### VIOLATION 3 — Using clearance infrastructure to build a candidate pool

**Rule broken:**
> **32 CFR § 117.10(a)(5) — Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 117, Section 10, Paragraph (a), Subparagraph (5)**
>
> *"The contractor will limit requests for determinations of eligibility for access to classified information to the **minimum number of employees and consultants necessary** for operational efficiency in accordance with contractual obligations and other requirements of this rule. Requests for determinations of eligibility for access to classified information **will not be used to establish a cache of cleared employees**."*

**Document:** 32 CFR Part 117 (NISPOM)  
**Section:** § 117.10(a)(5)  
**Direct URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(a)(5)

**What is violated:** Collecting SSNs from cleared candidates under the guise of "verifying clearance" — without an actual employment requirement behind each request — is precisely what this paragraph prohibits. Staffing firms that harvest SSNs from multiple cleared candidates "for future positions" are building an unauthorized cache, which the regulation explicitly bars.

---

### VIOLATION 4 — Collecting SSN outside the authorized federal system (Privacy Act)

**Rule broken:**
> **5 U.S.C. § 552a — The Privacy Act of 1974, as amended**
>
> *"No agency shall disclose any record...contained in a system of records...except pursuant to a written request by, or with the prior written consent of, the individual to whom the record pertains."*

> **Also cited within NISPOM at 32 CFR § 117.10(d)(1):**
> *"The use and disclosure by the U.S. Government, and by U.S. Government contractors operating systems of records on behalf of a U.S. Government agency to accomplish an agency function, of the information provided by the employee on the SF-86 is governed by the **Privacy Act of 1974, as amended**, and by the routine uses published by the USG in the applicable System of Records Notice."*

**Document:** United States Code, Title 5, Section 552a (Privacy Act of 1974)  
**Also cross-referenced in:** 32 CFR § 117.10(d)(1)  
**Direct URL (Privacy Act):** https://www.govinfo.gov/link/uscode/5/552a  
**Direct URL (NISPOM cross-reference):** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(d)

**What is violated:** SSN is a Privacy Act-protected record. Collecting it outside an authorized federal system of records, without a lawful purpose tied to an active employment/investigation action, constitutes unauthorized collection of a Privacy Act record. The SF-86 data — including SSN — is governed by the System of Records Notice (SORN) for the NBIS, not by any private collection process.

---

### VIOLATION 5 — SF-86 must go through eApp — not email, phone, or any other channel

**Rule broken:**
> **32 CFR § 117.10(d) — Title 32, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 117, Section 10, Paragraph (d)**
>
> *"The electronic version of the SF 86...must be completed in **e-QIP or its successor system** by the contractor employee and reviewed by the FSO or other contractor employee(s) who has (have) been specifically designated by the contractor to review an employee's SF 86."*

**Document:** 32 CFR Part 117 (NISPOM)  
**Section:** § 117.10 — "Determination of eligibility for access to classified information for contractor employees"  
**Paragraph:** (d) — "Procedures for completing the electronic version of the SF 86"  
**Direct URL:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/section-117.10#p-117.10(d)  
**eApp (e-QIP successor):** https://eapp.nbis.mil

**What is violated:** Any method of collecting SF-86 data — including SSN — that is not through the NBIS eApp portal is outside the authorized system. Email, phone, fax, DOD SAFE drop-off, paper form, or any other channel is not "e-QIP or its successor system." Collecting PII through unauthorized channels exposes the collector to Privacy Act liability and violates this paragraph directly.
